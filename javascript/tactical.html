Tactical Walking

Early in the process of getting started in to running, most of us acquire a determination to run only.  It's a personal triumph to complete that first mile, 30 minutes, whatever, without walking a single step.  That getting-started way of thinking may be getting in the way of people racing faster times.

Tactical walking is my term for the walking I do in a race.  As for any tactical manouver, there is a goal -- to complete the race as fast as possible.  In a recent 8k cross-country race I did a fair amount of tactical walking.  The result was that I finished ahead of a fellow who finished well ahead of me at 3000m on the track.  He walked less than I did, but I finished first.  (Overall, I was 13th of 46 in my age group.  I'm not talking about a strictly back of the pack tactic.)

As one goes out to ultramarathons, which I did this year for the first time, an 'easy' 50 km trail race, the amount of walking and fraction of people doing it goes up.
