Recomputing the sea ice history

    The original sea ice history available from here was constructed by saving each day's ice cover analysis as it was produced.  The production methods changed through time, resulting in better analyses for the day, but introducing artefacts when the resulting fields were considered for climatological purposes.

    Therefore, we are rebuilding the climate-oriented series with a fixed method and reference fields.
The current recomputed time series, beginning with October 1, 1995 and running to the present, is available in ftp://polar.wwb.noaa.gov/pub/ice/global/.  There is also a readme file present which describes the current level of processing and the support files used (in particular, the land mask, which is now notably different than the one previously used).  In time, this series will run self-consistently from late 1978 to the present.  It will be recomputed in its entirity whenever we learn of either an error in the processing (rare, we hope) or of better methods for analysis.

    In addition to the revised daily series, we are now computing integral quantities, such as monthly summaries and areas.  The daily areas may be viewed on the experimental analysis page, at http://polar.wwb.noaa.gov/seaice/expt.analy/Welcome.html.
